# Platforms for Authority Building

AI engines don't just crawl your website — they pull from a network of trusted third-party sources. Being present on these platforms is one of the highest-leverage AEO tactics.

---

## Top Picks

**[Wikipedia](https://www.wikipedia.org/) + [Wikidata](https://www.wikidata.org/)**
Wikipedia dominates ChatGPT citations at 47.9% of top sources. Wikidata is the structured layer behind it — a Wikidata entry with `sameAs` declarations is the most reliable signal for AI entity recognition. If your brand or category doesn't have a Wikipedia page, that's a gap worth addressing.

**[Reddit](https://reddit.com/)**
Top citation source for Perplexity and Google AI Overviews. Authentic participation in relevant subreddits (r/SaaS, r/ProductManagement, r/marketing, etc.) builds AI credibility at scale. The key word is authentic — low-effort promotion gets downvoted and ignored.

**[G2](https://www.g2.com/) / [Clutch](https://clutch.co/)**
AI systems actively check G2 when assembling SaaS recommendations and Clutch for agency/service recommendations. A complete, well-reviewed profile on the relevant platform is non-negotiable for B2B AEO. G2 is also among the top platforms cited in AI-generated SaaS comparisons.

## The pattern

Brands with 10+ independent domains referencing them achieve 3.2× higher AI mention rates. This isn't about being everywhere — it's about being present on the specific platforms AI engines treat as authoritative sources. Wikipedia, Wikidata, Reddit, and G2/Clutch cover the highest-priority ground for most brands.
