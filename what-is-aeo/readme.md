# What is AEO?

Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) is the practice of structuring and optimizing content so AI-powered answer engines can retrieve, understand, and cite it when answering user questions.

Where traditional SEO focused on ranking in a list of links, AEO is about becoming the source — the one ChatGPT quotes, that Perplexity cites, that Google AI Overviews surfaces above search results.

---

## Why it matters now

The numbers are hard to ignore:

- ChatGPT processes over 2 billion queries daily
- Google AI Overviews appear in ~55% of all US searches
- AI-referred sessions grew 527% year-over-year through mid-2025
- Over 65% of searches now end without a click

Users aren't browsing ten blue links — they're getting one synthesized answer from a handful of trusted sources. If your brand isn't in that handful, you're invisible for a growing share of searches.

Gartner projects traditional search volume will drop 25% by 2026 as users migrate to AI-powered answer tools.

---

## AEO vs SEO vs GEO

These disciplines work together, not against each other:

- **SEO** — Optimize for rankings in traditional SERPs. Success = rankings, clicks, organic traffic.
- **AEO** — Optimize for being cited in AI-generated answers. Success = citation rate, AI Share of Voice, brand sentiment.
- **GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)** — The umbrella term for all AI search visibility strategies. AEO is the answer-retrieval layer within GEO.

Strong SEO is a prerequisite for AEO. AI engines use live web search and Common Crawl, so content that isn't indexed won't be cited. But ranking #1 on Google doesn't guarantee ChatGPT will mention you.

---

## Core concepts

**Structured content** — Answer-first writing, direct answer blocks (40–80 word standalone paragraphs), FAQ schema, HowTo schema, and JSON-LD markup make content extractable by AI retrieval systems.

**Entity optimization** — AI engines map the world as knowledge graphs. Establishing your brand as a recognized entity via Wikidata, `sameAs` schema declarations, and consistent cross-platform descriptions is the foundation everything else builds on.

**E-E-A-T** — Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness. Named authors with verifiable credentials, cited sources, and corroboration across independent third-party domains are the core authority signals AI engines respond to.

**Citation density** — The number of independent domains that mention or reference your brand. Brands with 10+ independent citations achieve 3.2× higher AI mention rates.

**llms.txt** — A proposed standard file (like robots.txt) for communicating site content to LLM crawlers. Already implemented by Stripe, Zapier, Cloudflare, and Anthropic. Supported by OpenAI and Perplexity.

**Freshness** — Content under 3 months old is 3× more likely to be cited. Leading AEO brands update key pages quarterly.

**Zero-click optimization** — AEO accepts that users may not click through. Being cited in an AI response is brand-building even without a visit.

## AEO vs Traditional SEO at a Glance

| Dimension | Traditional SEO | AEO |
|---|---|---|
| **Target** | Search algorithm ranking | LLM retrieval and citation |
| **Success Metric** | Rankings, CTR, organic traffic | Citation rate, AI Share of Voice, brand mentions |
| **Content Format** | Keyword-targeted pages | Answer-first, extractable, entity-rich |
| **Authority Signal** | Backlinks, Domain Authority | Named authorship, independent citations, entity corroboration |
| **User Interaction** | Click → visit | Often zero-click — brand impression without visit |
| **Technical Layer** | Crawlability, indexation | Structured data, llms.txt, entity schema |

---

Also known as: **GEO** (Generative Engine Optimization), **LLMO** (Large Language Model Optimization), **AI SEO**, **LLM SEO**, **AISO** (AI Search Optimization), **AIO** (AI Optimization).
