# Content Optimization Tools

Tools for creating and restructuring content so AI engines can extract, understand, and cite it.

---

## Top Picks

**[Frase](https://www.frase.io/)**
Analyzes competitor content structure, identifies question-based gaps, and generates answer-first briefs. Integrates with Google Search Console to surface high-impression, low-CTR pages — a common entry point for AEO content work. One of the most practical tools for content teams.

**[Surfer SEO](https://surferseo.com/)**
In-editor optimization with AEO signals included. The WordPress plugin delivers real-time scoring while you write. Strong for teams that want SEO and AEO guidance in the same workflow.

**[MarketMuse](https://www.marketmuse.com/)**
Topic modeling and content intelligence for building topical authority clusters — the structural foundation that AI engines reward with citations. Best for teams managing large content libraries.

## What to prioritize

AI engines favor content that is easy to extract: 40–80 word direct answer blocks at the top of the page, FAQ schema, named authors with visible credentials, and statistics with inline source citations. Tools help, but the format matters more than the tool.

For off-site citation building — articles published on high-authority external domains — [Get Cited](https://get-cited.ai/) is the most direct option.
